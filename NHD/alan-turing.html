              Alan Turing

              "Alan Turing." - The Alan Turing Internet Scrapbook, 2006



              Born in London in 1912, Alan Turing lived a childhood of loneliness. Turing's parents traveled extensively, leaving him and his brother in the care of family friends.
              “The thing that stands out most in my mind was his integrity and his intelligence for a child so young as he then was.”
              - Ms. Thompson, Turing's childhood nanny
              Turing was very socially awkward, which alienated him from his peers. In his isolation, Turing pursued original ways of thinking.

              In 1926, Turing was accepted at Sherborne School, where he developed a love for mathematics. Later, he pursued this passion at King’s College from 1931 to 1934.
              “Turing was different; even at an early age. He had different intellectual interests. His high pitched stammer sounded different. His unwillingness to take the expected path was different. Five years in a British public boarding school did not transform him into the conformist that his teachers and parents wanted. Instead, he was fascinated by science and mathematics – fields in which he quickly made a lasting impact.”
              - (Beavan)

              Homosexuality


              "As he grew older, Turing became increasingly aware that he was 'different' from most men around him, down to his very genetic code. He accepted that he was a homosexual, and struggled to find a handful of friends who would accept him in turn."
              - (Henderson)
              Turing noticed that he was sexually fond of other men. While at Sherborne School, Turing found his first love interest in Christopher Morcom. Though they were simply friends, Turing's interest in Morcom was evident in his journal entries:

              During his life, Turing's homosexuality furthered his isolation. However, this isolation allowed him to think creatively, later helping him develop an original solution to an important computation problem.

              He was later arrested for his homosexuality, leading to his eventual suicide.
