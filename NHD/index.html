              Computers touch every aspect of our lives. From the clock to the car, the radio to the satellite, computers have forever changed society into what it is today. The computerized world we live in owes its origins to Alan Turing's revolutionary creation of a new form of computation; one that allowed machines to solve human problems.
              "Alan Turing was a complicated and eccentric genius – a man ahead of his time. In the 1930s, this British mathematician envisioned a digital world few others could imagine... Today, Turing is considered one of the 20th century’s most important people."
              – (Beavan)
              Being socially secluded for most of his life, Alan Turing found refuge in mathematics. Turing's isolation led him to develop unique methods for solving problems. Going against traditional thought, Turing combined the ideas of mathematics and machines to create the Turing Machine. This new type of computation converted complex problems into simple commands. During World War II, he was able to put his new creation to work, making crucial contributions in breaking German Enigma messages. His groundbreaking work now helps us perform daily tasks. This is the story of how Alan Turing, an unorthodox mathematician, developed a conceptual computer, the Turing Machine, which revolutionized computation.
